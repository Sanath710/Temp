Section 1

Executive Summary

Greenheck, a global leader in commercial HVAC systems and industrial ventilation solutions, operates a complex manufacturing and distribution network spanning multiple facilities, product lines, and geographic regions. As the organization has grown through organic expansion and strategic acquisitions, the underlying data infrastructure has evolved into a fragmented landscape of legacy data warehouses, departmental data marts, and siloed reporting tools that no longer serve the needs of a modern, data-driven enterprise.

This document presents the complete technical implementation plan for replacing the existing data infrastructure with SAP Datasphere as the unified, cloud-native enterprise data warehouse and analytics platform. The initiative, referred to internally as Project Helix, represents a strategic investment in Greenheck's data capabilities and is a foundational enabler for the company's broader digital transformation roadmap.

Strategic Context

Greenheck's current analytics environment comprises over 15 distinct data repositories, including a legacy SAP BW/4HANA system (version 2.0), multiple SQL Server-based data marts built for individual business functions, and numerous Excel-based reporting processes that persist across operations and finance. This fragmentation results in inconsistent metrics, delayed reporting cycles, excessive manual reconciliation effort, and an inability to perform cross-functional analytics that span the full value chain from raw material procurement through finished goods delivery.

The selection of SAP Datasphere as the target platform was the result of a rigorous evaluation process that assessed six enterprise data platform options against Greenheck's specific requirements for SAP-native integration, semantic layer richness, cloud scalability, total cost of ownership, and alignment with the existing SAP application landscape (SAP S/4HANA, SAP IBP, SAP SuccessFactors).

Project Helix encompasses the full lifecycle from requirements gathering through production deployment, covering six comprehensive use cases that collectively address the most critical analytics gaps identified during the discovery phase:

  • Order-to-Cash Analytics — Unified view of order processing, fulfillment, invoicing, and accounts receivable across all business units and distribution channels
  • Supply Chain Demand Planning — Integration of historical demand signals, forecast data from SAP IBP, and inventory positions to enable responsive supply chain decision-making
  • Manufacturing Performance — Real-time and historical analysis of production efficiency, quality metrics, downtime drivers, and capacity utilization across all manufacturing facilities
  • Product Costing and Margin Analysis — Granular cost decomposition from raw materials through finished goods, enabling product-level and customer-level profitability analysis
  • Customer 360 and Sales Analytics — Comprehensive customer profiling, order behavior analysis, and sales performance tracking across all channels and geographies
  • Financial Consolidation and Reporting — Automated financial data aggregation, intercompany elimination support, and multi-dimensional financial reporting aligned with corporate chart of accounts

6.1 Use Case 1: Order-to-Cash Analytics

6.1.1 Business Context

Order-to-Cash (O2C) is the most critical business process for Greenheck, representing the end-to-end flow from customer order placement through order fulfillment, delivery, invoicing, and payment receipt. Currently, analyzing the complete O2C cycle requires extracting data from three separate systems (SAP ECC for orders and invoices, a logistics system for delivery status, and a separate AR aging report from the finance data mart), resulting in a 3–5 day turnaround for any cross-functional O2C analysis.

6.1.2 Business Questions Addressed

  • What is the end-to-end cycle time from order receipt to payment collection, segmented by customer, product line, and channel?
  • Where are the bottlenecks in the order fulfillment process that extend cycle time?
  • What is the order-to-delivery fill rate by product line and plant, and how is it trending over time?
  • What is the revenue leakage from cancelled orders, returns, and pricing discounts?
  • What is the Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) by customer segment, and which accounts require attention?
  • How does actual order mix compare to forecast assumptions used for production planning?
